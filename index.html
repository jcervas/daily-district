How to Play

Identify the congressional district shown on the map using the 2026 district boundaries.

  • You have 6 guesses per day.
  • A new district appears every day — same for all players.
  • Click a state on the reference map to guess it first.
  • Once you nail the state, pick the district number.
  • Your timer starts when you submit your first guess.
  • Sign in to track your statistics and compare with other players.
  • Come back tomorrow for a new district!

Hot / Cold elimination

Each wrong state guess tells you something geographic:

  • Hot / Adjacent — the correct state borders the one you guessed. Only neighboring states stay in play.
  • Cold / Not adjacent — the correct state is far away. The guessed state and all its neighbors are eliminated.

Eliminated states grey out on the map and chip list automatically.

About Daily District

Daily District is an educational game that challenges players to identify all 435 U.S. House districts through geographic, map-based, and contextual clues. Created through a partnership between Dr. Jonathan Cervas of Carnegie Mellon University and Jason Fierman of the Redistrict Network, Daily District is an interactive way to learn about redistricting and how congressional districts shape American democracy, building civic knowledge and understanding for curious people of all backgrounds.

Daily District is committed to making civic education interactive, approachable, and fun. Donations to the Carnegie Mellon Institute for Strategy & Technology support research and help make educational projects like Daily District possible. Daily District is also seeking sponsors and organizational partners to support ongoing updates and maintenance of the game. If your organization is interested in sponsoring or partnering with Daily District, please contact RedistrictNetwork@gmail.com.
